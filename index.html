Come explore Northshore's conserved properties by the Trustees of Reservations and Greenbelt Essex County's Land Trust. Whether you want to hike, kayak, run, paint or bird, you’ll find the perfect spot at one of the reservations.

Reservations in Northshore

Agassiz Rock

Ascend to where a massive granite monolith juts into the sky, then pass a swamp where another huge boulder has sat as a silent witness for millennia.

Appleton Farm

Wander past crop fields and cow pastures at one of the country’s oldest continuously operating farms.

Castle Hill Estate

Experience the grandeur of a 2,100-acre seaside estate, its marvelously landscaped grounds, restored Grand Allée, gardens, and Casino complex.

Coolidge Point

A scenic pond and a small forest of pine and oak lead you to a spectacular seaside lawn offering memorable views of Massachusetts Bay and the North Shore.

Crane Beach

Soak up the seaside fun at one of the Northeast's most spectacular beaches, and follow trails and boardwalks through a spellbinding landscape of sand dunes and salt marsh.

Greenwood Farm

Wander through an open field and enjoy a memorable view of the historic Paine House, with the broad estuary of the Ipswich River extending beyond.

Halibut Point

Scramble across ledges scarred for eons by wind and wave, and examine tide pools teeming with marine life.

Long Hill

The elegant plantings and cultivated grounds of this public garden bloom with vibrancy that's more than a century in the making.

Old Town Hill

Climb to the top of this 168-ft coastal hill and enjoy panoramic views of the Great Marsh and New Hampshire's Isle of Shoals.

Ravenswood Park

Ten miles of crushed-stone carriage paths and trails meander through the park.

Stavros

Enjoy an easy ascent to a coastal hilltop that rewards you with panoramic views of Crane Beach and the Essex River estuary.

Sally Weatherall

Panoramic Marsh Views & Osprey Nest Observation. Spy on egrets and herons feeding on the vast salt marsh or observe migrating waterfowl.

Stony Cove

Jutting out at a bend in the Little River where it joins the Annisquam River, this jewel provides a beautiful entry to Gloucester along busy Route 128.

Harvey

Unparalleled views of Ipswich Bay, including Crane Beach and Plum Island, one of the best spots on Cape Ann to sit and watch the sunset.

Allyn Cox

Stunning views of a breathtaking New England salt marsh, with an open field, bordered by hedgerow on the edge of a tidal estuary in the Great Marsh ecosystem.

Tompson

Experience an exceptional example of Cape Ann’s unique rugged landscape with exposed granite bedrock in one of Greenbelt’s largest reservations.

The Trustees of Reservations is a non-profit land conservation and historic preservation organization dedicated to preserving natural and historical places in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. It is the oldest land conservation nonprofit organization of its kind in the world and has 140,000 dues-paying members as of 2018. -Wikipedia

Greenbelt is a member-supported nonprofit organization working to conserve local farmland, wildlife habitat and scenic landscape. A primary focus of our work is the creation of a network of greenbelts consisting of natural corridors and visually intact landscapes.
